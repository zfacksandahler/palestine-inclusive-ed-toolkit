# Glossary of Key Terms (English-Arabic)

This glossary provides definitions of essential terms related to disability studies and inclusive education in the Palestinian context. Each entry includes the term in English, its Arabic translation, and a concise definition in English.

---

## 1. Inclusive Education
**Arabic:** التعليم الدامج  
**Definition:** An educational approach that ensures all students, including those with disabilities, learn together in mainstream classrooms with appropriate support and accommodations.

## 2. Assistive Technology
**Arabic:** التكنولوجيا المساعدة  
**Definition:** Any device, software, or equipment that helps individuals with disabilities perform tasks, improve functional capabilities, and participate more fully in education and society.

## 3. Individualized Education Plan (IEP)
**Arabic:** خطة تعليمية فردية  
**Definition:** A written document developed for a student with disabilities that outlines specific educational goals, accommodations, and services tailored to the student's unique needs.

## 4. Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
**Arabic:** التصميم الشامل للتعلم  
**Definition:** A framework for designing instructional materials and activities that are accessible to all learners from the outset, providing multiple means of representation, engagement, and expression.

## 5. Disability
**Arabic:** إعاقة  
**Definition:** A physical, sensory, intellectual, or psychosocial condition that may interact with environmental barriers to hinder full and effective participation in society on an equal basis with others.

## 6. Accessibility
**Arabic:** إمكانية الوصول  
**Definition:** The degree to which environments, products, services, and information are usable by all people, including those with disabilities, without the need for adaptation.

## 7. Reasonable Accommodation
**Arabic:** تسهيلات معقولة  
**Definition:** Necessary and appropriate modifications or adjustments that do not impose a disproportionate burden, designed to ensure persons with disabilities can enjoy equal rights and freedoms.

## 8. Early Intervention
**Arabic:** التدخل المبكر  
**Definition:** Services and support provided to infants and young children with developmental delays or disabilities to enhance their development and prevent further difficulties.

## 9. Mainstreaming
**Arabic:** الدمج في التعليم العام  
**Definition:** The practice of integrating students with disabilities into regular classrooms for part or all of the school day, often with additional support.

## 10. Special Education
**Arabic:** التربية الخاصة  
**Definition:** Specially designed instruction, at no cost to parents, to meet the unique needs of a child with a disability, which may include separate classrooms or resource rooms.

## 11. Sensory Impairment
**Arabic:** ضعف حسي  
**Definition:** A disability that affects one or more senses, such as vision (visual impairment) or hearing (hearing impairment), impacting how information is received and processed.

## 12. Psychosocial Disability
**Arabic:** إعاقة نفسية اجتماعية  
**Definition:** Disabilities that arise from mental health conditions and the social environment, often involving challenges with emotional regulation, social interaction, and daily functioning.

---

*Note: Arabic translations are based on common usage in Palestinian educational contexts. Terminology may vary across regions.*
